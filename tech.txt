S.U.R.A.J. (Solar Utility & Radiance Analytical Judgment)
Technical Explanation Guide for Non-Technical Readers
================================================================================

Welcome to the S.U.R.A.J. project! This guide explains what this project is and how it works behind the scenes, using simple analogies so anyone can understand it—no coding experience required!

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART 1: THE "BRAIN" (The Machine Learning Pipeline)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Imagine you are trying to predict how sunny it will be tomorrow. To do this accurately, you'd look at past weather records, find patterns, and use those patterns to make a guess. This is exactly what our Machine Learning (ML) pipeline does using Python. It consists of 11 sequential steps (scripts):

1. 01_data_fetch.py (Gathering the Ingredients)
What it does: This script goes out to the internet (or a database) and downloads massive amounts of historical weather and solar data. 
Simple terms: It's like going to the grocery store to buy all the raw ingredients before you start cooking.

2. 02_data_preprocessing.py (Washing and Chopping)
What it does: Raw data is often messy—missing numbers, wrong formats, or duplicates. This script cleans everything up.
Simple terms: Washing the vegetables and chopping them so they are ready to be used in the recipe.

3. 03_eda.py (Tasting and Inspecting)
What it does: EDA stands for Exploratory Data Analysis. It creates graphs and charts so we can visually understand the trends (like noticing it's sunnier in July than December).
Simple terms: Looking at the ingredients to understand their flavor profiles before mixing them.

4. 04_feature_engineering.py (Creating the Secret Sauce)
What it does: It combines basic data into more useful clues. For example, it might combine "Time of Day" and "Cloud Cover" to create a new, highly predictive clue.
Simple terms: Mixing spices together to create a unique seasoning blend that makes the dish better.

5. 05_train_test_split.py (Setting Aside the Taste Test)
What it does: We split our data into two piles. We use 80% of it to "teach" the AI, and hide the remaining 20% to "test" the AI later to see if it actually learned or just memorized the answers.
Simple terms: Saving a small portion of your meal on the side so someone else can taste-test it later to see if you cooked it right.

6. 06_baseline_model.py (The Beginner's Guess)
What it does: It makes a very simple, naive prediction (like guessing that tomorrow will be exactly the same as today) to set a baseline score.
Simple terms: Taking a completely wild guess just to see if our advanced methods are actually doing a better job than random chance.

7. 07_random_forest.py (The Expert Panel)
What it does: This is the actual AI brain! A "Random Forest" is basically thousands of tiny, slightly different decision trees making predictions, and then they all vote on the final answer.
Simple terms: Instead of asking one person for the weather forecast, you ask 1,000 meteorologists and take the average of their answers.

8. 08_evaluation.py (Grading the Test)
What it does: This script compares the AI's predictions against the hidden 20% of data we saved earlier to grade how accurate the AI is.
Simple terms: The teacher grading the student's final exam.

9. 09_hyperparameter_tuning.py (Fine-Tuning the Dials)
What it does: The AI has settings (like knobs on a radio). This script automatically twists all the knobs until it finds the perfect frequency where the AI is the smartest.
Simple terms: Adjusting the oven temperature and baking time until the cake comes out perfectly moist.

10. 10_feature_importance.py (Finding the MVP)
What it does: It ranks the clues to see which ones the AI relied on the most. (Spoiler: Cloud cover is usually the biggest factor!).
Simple terms: Figuring out which ingredient was the "star" of the dish.

11. 11_actual_vs_predicted.py (The Final Report Card)
What it does: It creates a visual chart comparing what actually happened in the real world versus what the AI predicted would happen.
Simple terms: Putting the real photo of the weather next to your drawing of the weather to see how closely they match.


--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
PART 2: THE "FACE" (The Interactive Dashboard)
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The "Brain" is great, but it's just code and math. We built a beautiful, interactive "Face" (a web dashboard) so humans can easily play with the AI's logic. It lives in the `dashboard` folder.

1. dashboard/index.html (The Skeleton)
What it does: This file builds the structure of the website. It places the text, creates the sliding buttons for "Cloud Fraction" and "Temperature", and places the circular gauge on the screen.
Simple terms: The wooden frame and drywall of a house.

2. dashboard/styles.css (The Paint & Decor)
What it does: This file makes everything look beautiful. It gives the dashboard its dark, futuristic space theme, adds the glowing neon cyan and orange colors, and makes the sun icon pulse.
Simple terms: The interior design, paint, lighting, and furniture of the house.

3. dashboard/script.js (The Muscles & Nerves)
What it does: This file makes the website interactive. It listens to you moving the sliders. When you increase the "Cloud Cover" slider, this script acts like a mini-version of our AI: it does the math, realizes clouds block the sun, and tells the gauge to drop down and turn red.
Simple terms: The electricity and plumbing of the house—it makes the lights turn on when you flick the switch!
